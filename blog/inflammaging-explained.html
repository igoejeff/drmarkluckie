What Is Inflammaging?

The term "inflammaging" was coined by immunologist Dr. Claudio Franceschi in 2000. It describes a state of low-grade, chronic, systemic inflammation that persists as we age — not the acute inflammation that heals a cut or fights an infection, but a persistent, slow-burning inflammatory response that never fully turns off.

Acute inflammation is your body's emergency response team. A sprained ankle swells, turns red, gets hot — that's acute inflammation doing its job. Within days or weeks, it resolves. Inflammaging is the opposite: it's like a 911 call that never gets answered, leaving emergency sirens blaring 24 hours a day, seven days a week. That constant alarm state is what destroys you from the inside.

The Key Distinction: Acute inflammation saves your life. Chronic inflammation — inflammaging — ends it prematurely. Understanding the difference is the first step to fighting back.

How the Fire Starts

Inflammaging doesn't have a single cause — it's a perfect storm of modern living. Here are the primary ignition sources:

Ultra-Processed Food

Refined sugars, seed oils, artificial additives, and fast food trigger continuous inflammatory cascades in the gut and bloodstream.

Sleep Deprivation

Even one night of poor sleep spikes inflammatory cytokines. Chronically short sleep is one of the fastest pathways to inflammaging.

Chronic Stress

Cortisol, when chronically elevated, disrupts immune signaling and creates the perfect environment for inflammatory activity to flourish.

Sedentary Lifestyle

Physical inactivity dramatically increases inflammatory markers. Movement is anti-inflammatory medicine — and it's free.

Alcohol & Toxins

Alcohol is directly pro-inflammatory. Environmental toxins — from plastics, pesticides, and pollution — further burden the immune system.

Hormonal Decline

Declining testosterone and estrogen remove key anti-inflammatory protections. This is why inflammaging accelerates dramatically after 40.

11 Signs You Have Chronic Inflammation Right Now

Most people are walking around with chronic inflammation and have no idea. They chalk it up to "getting older" or "just being tired." Here are the warning signs your body is already on fire:

01

Persistent fatigue that sleep doesn't fix

Inflammatory cytokines directly interfere with cellular energy production and sleep quality, creating a vicious cycle of exhaustion.

02

Brain fog and difficulty concentrating

Neuroinflammation — inflammation in the brain — impairs memory, focus, and cognitive performance. Many call it "brain on fire."

03

Unexplained weight gain, especially around the belly

Visceral fat is both a cause and consequence of inflammation — it actively secretes inflammatory compounds called adipokines.

04

Frequent illness or slow recovery

A chronically activated immune system becomes exhausted and less effective at fighting actual threats.

05

Joint pain and stiffness

Especially in the morning or after sitting — a classic sign of low-grade inflammatory activity in the joints.

06

Skin problems: acne, eczema, psoriasis, premature wrinkles

The skin is the body's largest organ — and one of the first places chronic inflammation shows up visibly.

07

Digestive issues: bloating, gas, IBS symptoms

A leaky or inflamed gut is both a source and symptom of systemic inflammation — the two reinforce each other.

08

Mood disorders: anxiety and depression

Emerging research shows that chronic inflammation is a primary driver of depression — separate from serotonin imbalances.

09

High blood pressure or cholesterol

Inflammation damages arterial walls, triggering compensatory changes in blood pressure and lipid metabolism.

10

Low libido and sexual dysfunction

Inflammation suppresses testosterone production — which in turn increases inflammation. Another vicious cycle.

11

Elevated blood sugar or insulin resistance

Chronic inflammation impairs insulin receptor sensitivity, driving pre-diabetes and type 2 diabetes risk.

The Disease Connection: What Inflammaging Causes

This is where the stakes become crystal clear. Chronic inflammation is not just an annoyance — it is the root cause or a major contributor to virtually every chronic disease that kills people prematurely:

  • Cardiovascular disease: Inflammation damages arterial walls and drives plaque formation
  • Alzheimer's and dementia: Neuroinflammation is now considered central to cognitive decline
  • Cancer: Chronic inflammation creates an environment where abnormal cells thrive
  • Type 2 diabetes: Inflammatory molecules directly impair insulin function
  • Arthritis: Both osteo and rheumatoid — driven by inflammatory signaling
  • Asthma and COPD: Airway inflammation is the defining feature of both conditions
  • Autoimmune disease: A confused inflammatory system that attacks your own tissues

The Inflammaging Diet: Foods That Fuel the Fire

What you eat is either fighting inflammation or feeding it. Here's the breakdown:

Pro-Inflammatory Foods

  • Refined sugar and high-fructose corn syrup
  • Industrial seed oils (canola, soy, corn, sunflower)
  • Ultra-processed snack foods
  • White bread, pasta, and refined grains
  • Conventional red meat from factory farms
  • Margarine and trans fats
  • Artificial sweeteners and additives
  • Excessive alcohol

Anti-Inflammatory Foods

  • Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel — omega-3s)
  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale, arugula)
  • Berries (blueberries, strawberries, cherries)
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Turmeric with black pepper (curcumin)
  • Green tea and matcha
  • Nuts and seeds (walnuts, flaxseed)
  • Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower)
